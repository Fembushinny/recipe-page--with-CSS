Boiled Yam and Egg Sauce

Key ingredients: Egg, tomatoes, peppers, and onions

This is a simple and delicious recipe for boiled yam and egg sauce. It is a popular dish enjoyed for breakfast, lunch, or dinner.

Ingredients

  • White yam: ½ to 1 medium tuber, peeled and sliced
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon (to taste)
  • 2-3 medium tomatoes (chopped)
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers (chopped)
  • Seasoning: 1 bouillon cube, 1 tsp curry powder, 1 tsp thyme, and salt to taste

Instructions

    Boil the Yam
  1. Peel and slice the yam into 1-inch thick discs or rectangular chunks.
  2. Rinse thoroughly in cold water until it runs clear.
  3. Place in a pot with enough water to cover the slices, add salt, and boil for 15-20 minutes until fork-tender.
  4. Drain the water and set the yam aside.
    5. Make the Egg Sauce
  5. Whisk the eggs in a bowl with a pinch of salt until well combined.
  6. Heat oil in a frying pan over medium heat and sauté onions until translucent (about 3 minutes).
  7. Add the chopped tomatoes and peppers; fry for 5-10 minutes until the water reduces and the oil begins to float on top.
  8. Stirr in the bouillon cube, curry, and thyme.
  9. Pour in the whisked eggs. Let them sit for about a minute to set before gently stirring to create soft, chunky curds. Serve the hot egg sauce alongside or over the boiled yam.
    10. Serve and enjoy your meal!

Nutritional Facts

This dish provides carbohydrates from yam, protein from eggs, and essential vitamins from vegetables.

Variation Tip

For extra flavor, you can add diced bell peppers, garlic, ginger, or even leafy vegetables like spinach or fluted pumpkin (ugu) to the sauce.

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 60 minutes

Total Time: 70 minutes
